Coffee Bean Types

There are two main types of coffee beans, each with distinct characteristics, flavors, and uses.

Arabica

Arabica is the most widely cultivated type of coffee bean in the world, accounting for approximately 60-70% of global coffee production.

Characteristics

  • Delicate and complex flavor
  • Good balance of sweetness and acidity
  • Rich aroma
  • Relatively low caffeine content

Main Producing Regions

Cultivated in high-altitude regions such as Ethiopia, Colombia, Brazil, Guatemala, and Costa Rica.

Flavor Profile

Characterized by fruity acidity, floral aroma, and sweet aftertaste. Ideal for straight coffee and espresso.

Robusta

Robusta is the second most widely cultivated coffee bean variety. As the name suggests, it is resistant to diseases and pests, making it easy to cultivate.

Characteristics

  • Strong bitterness and robust flavor
  • High caffeine content (approximately 2x that of Arabica)
  • Easy to cultivate with high yields
  • Relatively affordable

Main Producing Regions

Cultivated in lowland, hot and humid regions such as Vietnam, Indonesia, Uganda, and Brazil.

Flavor Profile

Characterized by strong bitterness and body, with nutty and chocolate-like flavors. Commonly used in instant coffee and espresso blends.

Selection Tips

When choosing coffee beans, consider the following points:

  • Preferred Flavor: Choose Arabica if you like acidity, Robusta if you prefer bitterness
  • Usage: Arabica for straight coffee, Robusta is also an option for blends and espresso
  • Caffeine: Choose Arabica if you want to reduce caffeine intake
  • Price: Select according to your budget

For beginners, we recommend starting with Arabica to enjoy its delicate flavor and aroma.
